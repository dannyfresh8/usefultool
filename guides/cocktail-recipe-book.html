⭐ Classic Cocktails

Old Fashioned
🌸 Orange peel🧪 Old Fashioned / Rocks
📋 Ingredients:
2 oz Bourbon/Rye, 1 sugar cube, 2 dashes Angostura bitters, Orange peel, Ice
📝 Method:
Muddle sugar cube with bitters. Add bourbon and ice. Stir well. Express orange peel over glass and drop in.
Martini

📋 Ingredients:
2.5 oz Gin, 0.5 oz Dry Vermouth, Lemon twist or olive
📝 Method:
Fill mixing glass with ice. Add gin and vermouth. Stir 30 seconds. Strain into chilled glass. Garnish.

🌶️ DIY Syrups & Mixers

Simple Syrup
📋 Ingredients:
1 cup Sugar, 1 cup Water
💡 Rich syrup: 2:1 sugar to water
📝 Method:
Heat equal parts sugar and water until dissolved. Cool. Store fridge 2 weeks.
Honey Syrup
📋 Ingredients:
0.5 cup Honey, 0.5 cup Warm water
💡 Great for Bees Knees
📝 Method:
Whisk together until combined. Store refrigerated.
Ginger Syrup
📋 Ingredients:
0.5 cup fresh Ginger (sliced), 1 cup Sugar, 1 cup Water
💡 Use in Moscow Mules
📝 Method:
Simmer 20 min. Strain. Cool. Store fridge 2 weeks.
Orgeat (Almond)
📋 Ingredients:
1 cup Almond milk, 1 cup Sugar, 1 tsp Rose water, 0.5 tsp Orange flower
💡 Essential for Mai Tai
📝 Method:
Warm almond milk. Dissolve sugar. Add floral waters. Cool.
Grenadine
📋 Ingredients:
1 cup Pomegranate juice, 1 cup Sugar, 1 tsp Rose water
💡 Use for Shirley Temples, Tequila Sunrise
📝 Method:
Heat until dissolved. Cool. Real grenadine beats store-bought.
Sour Mix
📋 Ingredients:
1 cup Lemon juice, 1 cup Lime juice, 1 cup Simple syrup
💡 Perfect for Margaritas, Whiskey Sours
📝 Method:
Combine and refrigerate. Use within 1 week.
Cinnamon Syrup
📋 Ingredients:
4 Cinnamon sticks, 1 cup Sugar, 1 cup Water
💡 Amazing in Old Fashioneds
📝 Method:
Simmer 15 min. Remove sticks. Cool. Store fridge.

✨ Bartending Pro Tips

  • Always chill your glass before making cocktails.
  • Fresh citrus juice is non-negotiable for quality drinks.
  • Stir spirit-only drinks (Manhattan, Martini). Shake anything with citrus, dairy, or eggs.
  • Measure precisely — cocktails are chemistry.
  • Store syrup refrigerated and use within 2 weeks.
  • Ice quality matters — use filtered water for clear ice.
