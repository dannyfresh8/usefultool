Best Investment Apps for Beginners 2026

📅 Updated: April 2026 · 📖 8 min read

You can start investing with as little as $1 today. Here's the best app for every type of investor.

Robinhood — Best for Active Traders

Minimum: $0 | Fees: $0 commissions | Best for: People who want to pick individual stocks

Robinhood made investing free and popular with a slick mobile app. Buy stocks, ETFs, and crypto with zero commission. The interface is simple and intuitive. Downsides: it encourages frequent trading, and customer support is weak. Good for active traders who know what they're doing.

Vanguard — Best for Long-Term Investors

Minimum: $1 (for digital advisor) | Fees: 0.03% ETF expense ratios | Best for: Buy-and-hold index investors

Vanguard invented index funds. Their expense ratios are the lowest in the industry. If you want to buy VOO, VTI, or VT and hold for decades, Vanguard is the most cost-effective choice. The app is basic but functional. Perfect for serious investors who don't need flashy features.

Fidelity — Best All-Around

Minimum: $0 | Fees: $0 commissions, 0% expense ratio on their index funds | Best for: Everyone

Fidelity offers the best combination of low fees, great customer service, and powerful tools. Their zero-fee index funds (FZROX, FZILX) charge literally nothing. The app is excellent. They also offer a 2% cashback credit card that can auto-invest rewards. Hard to beat Fidelity for most people.

Acorns — Best for Passive Savers

Minimum: $0 | Fees: $3/month | Best for: People who want hands-off investing

Acorns rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change. It's the "set it and forget it" option. You also get found money (cashback from partner brands) and financial education. The $3/month fee is high for small balances but includes retirement and checking accounts.

Betterment — Best Robo-Advisor

Minimum: $0 | Fees: 0.25%/year | Best for: Hands-off investors who want optimization

Betterment manages your portfolio automatically based on your goals and risk tolerance. It handles tax-loss harvesting, automatic rebalancing, and goal tracking. You answer a questionnaire and the algorithm does the rest. Great for people who want professional management without the human cost.

SoFi Invest — Best for Banking + Investing

Minimum: $0 | Fees: $0 | Best for: All-in-one finance

SoFi combines banking, investing, loans, and insurance in one app. You can buy stocks and ETFs with zero fees, or use their automated investing. Bonus features include IPO access and crypto trading. Best if you want one app for everything financial.

Which One Should You Pick?

  • Want to pick stocks? Robinhood or Fidelity
  • Want index funds forever? Vanguard
  • Want hands-off automation? Acorns or Betterment
  • Want one app for everything? SoFi
  • Want the best overall? Fidelity
