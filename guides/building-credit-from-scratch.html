Building credit with no history requires starting small and being consistent. Here's exactly how to build a solid credit score from nothing.

Step 1: Get a Secured Card

A secured credit card requires a cash deposit that becomes your credit limit. Put down $200-500. Use it for one small purchase monthly and pay the full balance before the due date. After 6-12 months of on-time payments, most issuers upgrade you to an unsecured card and return your deposit.

Step 2: Become an Authorized User

Ask a parent or trusted friend to add you as an authorized user on their credit card. You get a card and the account's positive history on your credit report — but the primary holder is responsible for payment. Choose someone with a long history of on-time payments and low credit utilization.
