Best Side Hustles for Beginners: Make Extra Money in 2026

📅 Updated: April 2026 · 📖 10 min read

Looking to make extra money? These side hustles are beginner-friendly, require little to no startup cost, and can earn you $200–$5,000/month.

1. Food Delivery (Uber Eats, DoorDash)

Earning potential: $15–$25/hour
Time commitment: Flexible — work when you want
Startup cost: A car or bike

One of the easiest side hustles to start. Sign up, turn on the app, and start delivering. Great for evenings and weekends. You can easily make $500–$1,000/month working 10–15 hours per week.

2. Freelance Writing

Earning potential: $50–$200/article
Time commitment: 3–10 hours/week
Startup cost: $0

Companies need content for their blogs and websites. Start on platforms like Upwork or Fiverr. Write about topics you know. As you build a portfolio, you can charge more. Experienced writers earn $100+/hour.

3. Virtual Assistant

Earning potential: $15–$35/hour
Time commitment: 5–20 hours/week
Startup cost: $0

Help business owners with email, scheduling, social media, and admin tasks. Skills like organization and basic software knowledge are all you need. Demand is high and growing.

4. Online Tutoring

Earning potential: $20–$60/hour
Time commitment: Flexible
Startup cost: $0

Teach English, math, coding, or any subject you know. Platforms like VIPKid, Chegg, and Wyzant connect you with students. If you speak English fluently, English tutoring alone can earn you $20+/hour.

5. Sell on Etsy

Earning potential: $100–$3,000/month
Time commitment: 5–15 hours/week
Startup cost: Low (materials or digital products)

Sell digital products (planners, templates, printables) or handmade items. Digital products are especially profitable — create once, sell forever. No inventory or shipping needed.

6. Pet Sitting & Dog Walking

Earning potential: $15–$30/hour
Time commitment: Flexible
Startup cost: $0

Use Rover or Wag to find local clients. Dog owners happily pay for walks, sitting, and boarding. If you love animals, this doesn't feel like work.

7. TaskRabbit

Earning potential: $20–$50/hour
Time commitment: Flexible
Startup cost: Basic tools

Get paid for assembling furniture, moving help, cleaning, and handyman tasks. Rates vary by location and skill level. Great if you're handy.

8. Rideshare Driving

Earning potential: $15–$30/hour
Time commitment: Flexible
Startup cost: A car

Drive for Uber or Lyft. Best in busy cities during peak hours. You can drive whenever you want — early mornings, weekends, or late nights.

9. Social Media Management

Earning potential: $300–$2,000/month per client
Time commitment: 3–10 hours/week per client
Startup cost: $0

Small businesses need help with Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook. If you know how to create content and engage followers, you can help multiple clients.

10. Airbnb Hosting

Earning potential: $500–$5,000+/month
Time commitment: 5–10 hours/week
Startup cost: A spare room or property

Rent out a spare room or entire property on Airbnb. Popular in tourist areas or cities with events. Even renting occasionally can generate significant income.

💡 Tip: Start with ONE side hustle and master it before adding another. Trying to do everything at once leads to burnout.
